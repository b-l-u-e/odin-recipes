Carrot cake pancakes

These pancakes are a great way to use up leftover carrots. They are not too sweet and are perfect for a weekend breakfast.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups coarsely grated carrots
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans or walnuts
  • Butter, for cooking
  • Maple syrup, for serving

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, cinnamon, ginger, and salt.
  2. In another bowl, whisk together the milk, egg, melted butter, and vanilla.
  3. Stir the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients until just combined.
  4. Stir in the carrots and nuts.
  5. Heat a large skillet or griddle over medium heat and coat with some butter.
  6. Spoon 1/4 cupfuls of batter onto the skillet and cook until bubbles appear on the surface.
  7. Flip and cook until browned on the other side.
  8. Repeat with the remaining batter.
  9. Keep the pancakes warm in a low oven while you cook the remaining pancakes.
  10. Serve with maple syrup.
